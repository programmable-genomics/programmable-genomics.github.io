Are you smarter than a design algorithm?

In the Programmatic Genomics Laboratory (PGL), we use deep learning models to predict characteristics of DNA sequences, and then use design methods to iteratively edit sequences to until the models predict our desired characteristics. Sometimes, the edits chosen by these procedures are surprising.

Try it yourself to build your intuition: click any nucleotide below to cycle through A → C → G → T and watch the "neural network" evaluate your design in real time. Right-click any edited nucleotide to restore it to its original base, or use the "reset" button to undo all edits. Note: for now, there is no neural network, only a PWM scan.

Objective: Maximize the score by editing the sequence to contain GATA1 binding motifs (MA0035.2). The score of each motif is derived from a position weight matrix (PWM) derived from JASPAR2026, with only matches above a score counting as hits. But avoid using too many edits! Each edit subtracts a point from your score. Underlined positions indicate a PWM hit with the pink bars identifying high information content positions and gray bars indicating the flanks.
